# Portfolio Direction: The Handmade Studio

## The Insight

The screenshots Ryan shared reveal his actual taste better than any concept I've proposed:
- A mountain photo with handwritten "me →" and "2500M" annotations
- Loewe's Chinese logotype with sketched arrows analyzing the curves, annotated "curve?", "same?", "width?"
- Both feel like pages from a personal notebook. Unpolished. Studious. Human.

This is the portfolio: **a designer's notebook made digital.**

Not a tech demo. Not a template with GSAP animations. Not an interactive canvas.
A portfolio that feels like you're flipping through someone's sketchbook — where
the process, the annotations, the half-finished thoughts are visible alongside
the polished work.

## What This Means for the Site

### The feeling:
- Handwritten annotations overlaying clean typography
- Sketch-like elements (arrows, circles, underlines) that look drawn, not coded
- A mix of polished work and raw process shots
- The layout feels loose, organic — not a rigid grid
- Things feel placed by hand, not by CSS Grid
- Warm paper-like background, not pure black or white
- The imperfection IS the design

### What it is NOT:
- Not a framer template with smooth animations
- Not a Three.js tech demo
- Not a dark void with particles
- Not a card grid
- Not a San Rita map

### References:
- Personal notebooks / sketchbooks
- Virgil Abloh's "3% approach" — small interventions on existing things
- Loewe brand annotations
- Studio Instagram accounts that show WIP process
- Handwritten wayfinding in Japanese architecture
- Korean craft traditions (connecting to Gyeol)
