Three approaches for the project index — all within the cooler cinematic palette. Click the one that resonates most.

Approach A Recommended
Cinematic scroll — Okada-inspired
Each project is a full-viewport scene. Scroll drives you through them like chapters of a film. Parallax depth on images, titles reveal on entry, progress dots on the side. Inspired by Tomoya Okada's scroll-linked storytelling.

Strengths

  • Most cinematic — each project is an experience
  • Parallax and scroll-driven reveals feel premium
  • Natural progression (scroll = advance)
  • Strong alignment with Okada's approach

Trade-offs

  • Requires hero images for every project
  • Slower browsing — can't quickly scan all projects
  • Complex scroll-jacking can feel heavy
Approach B
Horizontal slider — Cathydolle-inspired
Projects arranged as horizontal cards you swipe/drag through. Each card shows a cinematic hero image with metadata overlay. Next card peeks from the right. Drag or arrow keys to navigate. Like flipping through film stills.

Strengths

  • Direct Cathydolle lineage — proven pattern
  • Horizontal = distinctly cinematic (film strip feel)
  • Drag interaction feels tactile and engaging
  • Peek of next card creates anticipation

Trade-offs

  • Horizontal scroll can feel unnatural on desktop
  • Also requires hero images for every project
  • No quick overview of all projects at once
Approach C
Hybrid — list index with cinematic preview
Split layout: text list on the left (scannable), cinematic preview panel on the right that updates as you navigate the list. Best of both — you can quickly scan projects while getting a visual moment for each. Clicking enters the case study.
